We can't BFS or DFS over ùîä. A DFS would simply get stuck on the leftmost branch forever and a BFS would never reach depth 2. How then are we to traverse it?

In the infinite complete binary tree, nodes are uniquely indentified by a finite length binary string. In ùîä, nodes are uniquely indentified by a finite sequence of natural numbers. Let s(v) be v's corresponding sequence. In ùîä, u is the parent of v iff s(u)'s length is one less than s(v)'s and s(u) is a prefix of s(v).

Any tree traversal produces a well order on the tree's vertices. BFS on the complete infinite binary tree is the shortlex ordering (sort first by length, then lexographically). In fact, on level i, the set of corresponding binary strings is the set of all i-bit natural numbers, and the nodes are visited in increasing order.
